Definition
What is a
Pallet Network?
A shared delivery system for palletised freight. Independent hauliers share trailer space through a central hub-and-spoke network — so nobody sends a truck half-empty.

This can all happen same day, next day, or within 48 hours — depending on the service type of the job (Same Day, Next Day, or Economy). Each depot unload is a distinct leg of the order in Qargo.

Key Players
Who's Involved?
The Network
Runs the hub, sets standards, manages IT, handles billing. e.g. Palletways, Pall-Ex, Palletline.
Member Hauliers
Independent companies covering local postcodes. They collect, trunk, and deliver. Boots on the ground.
Shippers / Customers
Businesses sending palletised goods. Book through a member for nationwide delivery at a fraction of the cost.

The Market
Major UK Pallet Networks
Palletways
Europe's largest pallet network. 450+ members across 24 countries. Multiple UK hubs.
Pall-Ex
Major UK network with a state-of-the-art hub in Coalville, Leicestershire. ~100 UK members.
Palletline
Member-owned cooperative. ~70 members with strong regional coverage. Central hub in Minworth.
TPN
The Pallet Network — known for competitive pricing and broad membership. Hub in Burton-upon-Trent.
Palletforce
Part of EV Cargo. ~120 members operating via a hub in Burton-upon-Trent.
UPN
United Pallet Network. Member-owned with ~130 members across UK & Ireland. Hub in Corby.
Hazchem
The UK's only ADR pallet & parcel network — specialising in hazardous and chemical goods. Hub in Hinckley, Leicestershire.
Pallet-Track
Shareholder-owned network with 90+ members. Central hub in Wolverhampton, plus regional hubs in Wigan and Welwyn Garden City.
Pallet Xpress
Ireland’s leading pallet network, founded in 2004. ~26 member hauliers provide next-day island-wide coverage from a central hub in Huntstown Business Park, Dublin, plus an express service linking Ireland and the UK.
Many Qargo customers are members of one or more of these networks.
Challenges
Where It Gets Hard
Pallet networks face real operational challenges — and this is exactly where technology like Qargo adds value.
Visibility
Tracking pallets across multiple independent companies is hard. Shippers want real-time visibility, but data passes through many different systems.
Complexity
Managing trunking schedules, hub capacity, member performance, and customer expectations simultaneously requires serious coordination.
Legacy Systems
Many networks and members still rely on outdated software, manual processes, or phone/email-based booking — creating inefficiency and errors.
Scaling
As members grow, they need to handle more pallets, routes, customers, and data — without the headcount to match. Automation becomes essential.

Reference
Key Terms You'll Hear
Trunking
The long-haul leg between a member's depot and the central hub.
Collection Leg
The local route picking up pallets from shippers.
Delivery Leg
The local route dropping off pallets to consignees.
Hub Sort
The overnight process of sorting pallets by destination.
POD
Proof of Delivery — confirming a pallet reached its recipient.
Member
An independent haulier who has joined the pallet network.
Consignment
A shipment of one or more pallets going to the same place.
Surcharges
Additional supplementary charges applied to a job — such as timed delivery (AM or PM), or if the job requires a tail-lift.
