Definition
What is a
Pallet Network?
A shared delivery system for palletised freight. Independent hauliers share trailer space through a central hub-and-spoke network — so nobody sends a truck half-empty.
🤝
Cooperative Model
Dozens of independent hauliers join as members
🏗️
Central Hub
Pallets sorted & redistributed each night

Key Players
Who's Involved?
🏢
Network Operator
Runs the hub, sets standards, manages IT, handles billing. e.g. Palletways, Pall-Ex, Palletline.
🚛
Member Hauliers
Independent companies covering local postcodes. They collect, trunk, and deliver. Boots on the ground.
📦
Shippers / Customers
Businesses sending palletised goods. Book through a member for nationwide delivery at a fraction of the cost.

The Market
Major UK Pallet Networks
Palletways
Europe's largest. 450+ members, 24 countries
Pall-Ex
State-of-the-art hub in Coalville. ~100 UK members
Palletline
Member-owned co-op. ~70 members
TPN
Competitive pricing. Hub in Burton-upon-Trent
Fortec
Larger freight & part-loads. Hub in Aldridge
Palletforce
~120 members via Burton-upon-Trent hub
Many Qargo customers are members of one or more of these networks.
